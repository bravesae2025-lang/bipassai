Best Free AI Humanizer in 2025 — Ranked and Reviewed

June 2025 7 min read By Bipass AI

Every AI humanizer claims to make your text "100% undetectable." None of them actually do that 100% of the time. What matters is which ones move the needle the most — and which ones are genuinely free vs. technically free but practically useless without a paid plan.

We tested the five most popular AI humanizers — Bipass AI, Undetectable.ai, BypassAI, QuillBot, and StealthWriter — on 500-word ChatGPT essays, running the results through Turnitin and GPTZero before and after. Here's the honest breakdown.

What Makes a Good AI Humanizer?

Before the rankings, here's what we actually measured:

  • Detection pass rate — What percentage of humanized outputs scored under 20% AI on Turnitin and GPTZero
  • Writing quality — Does the humanized output still make sense and read naturally?
  • Writing level control — Can you adjust the formality/level to match your actual writing?
  • How free the free tier actually is — Word limit, features locked behind paywall
  • Unique features — Anything beyond just the humanize button

2Undetectable.ai

Good — But Pricey

Undetectable.ai is one of the most well-known AI humanizers and it does a solid job on standard essays. It shows you the detection score from multiple detectors (Turnitin, GPTZero, Copyleaks, ZeroGPT) before and after humanizing, which is genuinely useful.

The free tier is extremely limited though — you get about 250 words per humanize on the free plan, and most features require a paid subscription starting at around $9.99/month. For a single essay it's barely enough. For ongoing use, you're paying every month whether or not you have anything to humanize.

    Pros
  • Shows multi-detector scores
  • Good detection pass rate
  • Well-established, large user base
    Cons
  • Free tier limited to ~250 words
  • Subscription required for real use
  • No writing level control
  • No auto-typing or revision history feature

3BypassAI

Solid — Subscription Required

BypassAI (bypassai.com) is a direct humanizer competitor with a clean interface. It produces good-quality humanized output for most essay types. The free trial gives you a limited word count to try it out, but ongoing use requires a paid subscription.

What BypassAI doesn't have: any kind of writing level control, custom style training, or revision history tool. It's a one-trick humanizer — good at that one trick, but no additional layer of protection.

    Pros
  • Clean, fast interface
  • Decent humanization quality
  • Free trial available
    Cons
  • Subscription required (monthly)
  • No writing level control
  • No style training or custom voice
  • No auto-type or revision history

4QuillBot

Paraphraser — Not a Humanizer

QuillBot is not an AI humanizer — it's a paraphraser. The distinction matters. QuillBot rewrites your text by swapping words and restructuring sentences. It does not address the statistical writing patterns that Turnitin's AI detector is actually flagging. Paraphrased AI text often still scores 50–70% AI on Turnitin because the underlying sentence structure and token probability distribution hasn't changed — just the surface words.

QuillBot is useful for plagiarism (changing wording from a source) but not for AI detection (changing the statistical writing pattern). Don't use it as your main defense against Turnitin's AI check.

    Pros
  • Free tier is generous (500 words)
  • Excellent for plagiarism avoidance
  • Well-known, trusted brand
    Cons
  • Does not reliably beat AI detection
  • No humanize model — just paraphrase
  • No writing level or style control

5StealthWriter

Mixed Results

StealthWriter is a newer humanizer with a free tier. Results were inconsistent in testing — some essays scored well, others barely moved the needle from the original AI score. The interface is simple and the free tier gives you a few thousand characters to try. But the lack of writing level control means you have no way to tune the output to sound like you specifically.

    Pros
  • Free tier available
  • Simple to use
    Cons
  • Inconsistent results
  • No writing level or style control
  • No revision history feature
  • Smaller user base, less tested

The Hidden Factor: Writing Level Control

Every tool above can humanize text to some degree. The thing that separates a good humanizer from a great one is writing level control.

ChatGPT defaults to a formal academic register — "Furthermore, it is imperative to consider the multifaceted implications..." No real undergraduate writes like that. When you humanize AI text without adjusting the level, you get a slightly less AI-sounding version of the same formal register. It still doesn't sound like you.

If your professor has read three of your essays and you suddenly submit one that sounds like a law review article, that's more suspicious than a 60% Turnitin score.

Bipass AI's writing level slider is the feature that addresses this. Set it to "student" or "beginner" and the humanized output matches the natural register of how a real student at that level writes. Combined with the style training feature (paste in old essays you've written), you can get output that sounds genuinely like you — not like a generic AI humanizer output.

The Auto Typer Advantage

The other feature that sets Bipass AI apart from all other humanizers is the Auto Typer Chrome extension. This is relevant because of how Turnitin's deeper integration works.

When you submit a Google Doc through Google Classroom or certain LMS integrations, Turnitin can access the document's revision history. A 1,500-word essay that appears fully formed in 45 seconds at midnight is a red flag — even if the text itself is clean. The Auto Typer solves this by typing your humanized text character by character into Google Docs over 10–15 minutes, creating a realistic revision history with natural pauses and edit patterns.

No other humanizer on this list has anything like this. It's the layer of protection that comes after the text itself is clean.
