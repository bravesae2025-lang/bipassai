01

How GPTZero actually detects AI

GPTZero scores your writing on two things: perplexity and burstiness. Perplexity is how predictable your word choices are. Burstiness is how much your sentence lengths vary.

AI text is low on both — every word is the safe, predictable choice and every sentence is a similar length. Humans write in bursts: a long sentence, then a short one, with surprising word choices. GPTZero highlights the sentences that look too smooth.

The signal Low perplexity + low burstiness = AI. To pass, you need both numbers to go up.

FAQ

GPTZero Detection Questions

Does GPTZero detect ChatGPT?
Yes. GPTZero was built specifically to spot text from ChatGPT, Gemini and other models. It highlights the exact sentences it thinks are AI and gives an overall human-vs-AI verdict.
What are perplexity and burstiness?
Perplexity is how predictable your word choices are — AI scores low because it always picks the safe word. Burstiness is how much your sentence lengths vary — humans mix long and short, AI stays uniform. GPTZero flags low scores on both.
How does Bipass AI help?
It rewrites your text to read like a real student wrote it — mixing long and short sentences and using less predictable wording — so it doesn't read like obvious AI. No tool can promise what GPTZero will say, but text that genuinely reads human is far less likely to stand out.
Which level works best against GPTZero?
The Student level is the most reliable for essays. If specific sentences still get highlighted, bump to a heavier level or use Custom to add more sentence variation.
Does it keep my essay's meaning?
Yes. Your points and argument stay the same — only the phrasing and rhythm change. You can review every change before submitting.
How long should my text be?
GPTZero is more confident on longer passages, so run a full essay rather than one paragraph. Bipass AI handles long text in a single pass without cutting it short.
