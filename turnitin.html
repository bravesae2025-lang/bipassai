01

Why Turnitin thinks your essay is AI

Turnitin's AI detector doesn't look for plagiarism — it looks for predictability. AI text from ChatGPT or Gemini is statistically smooth: even sentence lengths, polished vocabulary, and word choices that are easy to predict.

Real students don't write like that. They vary sentence length, repeat themselves, use simpler words, and make small mistakes. Turnitin scores how "human-random" your writing is — and flags anything too clean.

The signal Low variation in sentence rhythm + advanced vocabulary = high AI score. That's the pattern you need to break.

FAQ

Turnitin AI Detection Questions

Does Turnitin detect ChatGPT?
Yes. Turnitin has a dedicated AI writing detector separate from its plagiarism checker. It gives your document an AI percentage based on how predictable and uniform the writing is — and ChatGPT text scores high by default.
Why does Turnitin think my essay is AI?
Because it's too consistent. AI writes with even sentence lengths and polished vocabulary, which is statistically smoother than how humans write. That smoothness is the exact signal Turnitin scores.
How does Bipass AI help me pass Turnitin?
It rewrites your text at a real writing level — varying sentence rhythm, swapping over-polished words, and adding the small natural imperfections a student actually makes. That breaks the uniform pattern the detector relies on.
Which level is best for Turnitin?
For academic essays, the Student level is the most believable. It keeps your meaning intact while adding enough human variation to read naturally. Use Custom if you want to control exactly what changes.
Will it change the meaning of my essay?
No. Bipass AI keeps your argument and points the same — it only adjusts how the text reads. You can review every change before you submit and undo categories you don't want.
Is using Bipass AI against the rules?
Bipass AI is a writing tool. How you use it is your responsibility — always follow your school's academic integrity policy. Many students use it to make their own AI-assisted drafts read in their natural voice.
